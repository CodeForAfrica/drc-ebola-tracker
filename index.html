Contributors, Data & Methods

Data source

All DRC figures — national headline and every health zone — are transcribed from INSP situation reports (SitRep N°051, 04 July 2026, Table II). Uganda figures are from the Uganda Ministry of Health Ebola portal (05 July 2026). WHO Disease Outbreak News (DON608, 17 Jun) is retained only as a cross-reference.

WHO DON608 — Ebola disease caused by Bundibugyo virus, DRC & Uganda (19 June 2026)

DRC figures are as of 04 July 2026 (INSP SitRep N°051); Uganda figures are as of 05 July 2026 (Uganda Ministry of Health). The headline total is a composite of these two sources.

Method

  • The map is a choropleth: each health-zone polygon is shaded by confirmed cases on a sequential red ramp (light = few, dark = many). Click a shaded zone for its detail; hover to highlight it.
  • Each zone polygon is interactive: hovering highlights it and shows its figures, and clicking opens the zone's snapshot. Uganda (Kampala) is the only point marker, on a separate context layer.
  • All three affected provinces — Ituri, North Kivu and South Kivu — are now mapped at health-zone level from INSP SitRep N°051 (04 Jul 2026); the earlier North and South Kivu province context markers have been retired. Only Uganda (Kampala Metro) remains a separate context marker, as it is reported by a different source. 17 DRC cases were awaiting health-zone distribution at the time of reporting.
  • Zone polygons are approximate health-zone boundaries for visual placement, not official administrative boundaries.
  • The per-zone Trends tab shows cumulative confirmed cases and deaths from successive INSP SitReps (14 May–04 Jul 2026).
  • DRC case-fatality is provisional — confirmed deaths ÷ confirmed cases (506/1561 ≈ 32.4%, INSP SitRep N°051). Not comparable to earlier DONs (different case definition) and unstable while most cases remain unresolved.
