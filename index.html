Contributors, Data & Methods

Data source

All epidemiological figures are transcribed from the World Health Organization Disease Outbreak News:

WHO DON608 — Ebola disease caused by Bundibugyo virus, DRC & Uganda (19 June 2026)

DRC figures are as of 17 June 2026; Uganda figures as of 18 June 2026, including the Ituri health-zone breakdown (DON608, 17 June). WHO cites the Centre des opérations d'urgences de santé publique (COUSP-DRC) as the underlying DRC data source.

Method

  • The map is a proportional-symbol layer: each circle is a DRC health zone reporting confirmed Bundibugyo cases across Ituri, North Kivu and South Kivu. Circle area is proportional to confirmed cases (square-root scale) and colour follows a sequential red ramp. The most-affected zones are labelled with their count (e.g. Bunia 296, Mongbwalu 237, Rwampara 225).
  • Markers are shown in two tiers: the nine zones with 20 or more confirmed cases appear as larger, numbered, fully-opaque circles you can click for zone detail; the remaining zones appear as small faded dots ('also reporting') that show their figures on hover only.
  • All three affected provinces — Ituri, North Kivu and South Kivu — are now mapped at health-zone level from INSP SitRep N°39 (22 Jun 2026); the earlier North and South Kivu province context markers have been retired. Only Uganda (Kampala Metro) remains a separate context marker, as it is reported by a different source. 17 DRC cases were awaiting health-zone distribution at the time of reporting.
  • Marker coordinates are approximate health-zone centroids for visual placement, not official boundaries.
  • The trend chart uses cumulative confirmed counts from successive WHO DONs (21 May, 8 June, 10 June, 17 June).
